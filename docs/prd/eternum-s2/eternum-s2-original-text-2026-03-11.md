ETERNUM S2 SCOPE SUMMARY

Adjusted Game Speed

Problem: Seasons 0 and 1 of Eternum lasted a maximum of 2 weeks, with the result of the game being decided by collusion
between the top tribes after just over a week of gameplay. Top players committed several hours a day to play optimally,
resulting in burn-out and exhaustion. The fast seasons have also meant that new players hearing about the game through
socials arrived to a concluded event, with no timeline for a new season.

Solution: It is suggested that all game systems are revised for their rate of play and required user interaction, the
target should be that near-optimal play can be achieved with two logins per day (12 hour production/stamina cycles) and
5-10 mins of gameplay per Realm.

Requirements: Balance overhaul (config), including addition of essence ✅ Adjust wheat/fish for travel/explore to 3 per
troop. Adjust base storage to 1.2m kg, each storehouse is +1.2m kg storage. Introduction of an upkeep system and other
sinks ✅ Automation overhaul Adjusted victory conditions 25,000,000 Ancient Fragments to be sent to HSF to initialise
the build phase. New min/max resource requirement brackets in the balance sheet. New labor requirement to contribute
resources (1 labor/10 resource units). 550,000 VP (25,000 per resource) distributed to contributors on VP completion. 7
VP per second to shareholders of a completed HS. 12,000,000 VP to end the game. The tribal leaderboard is the primary
prize pool for the season, positioning is determined by the total cumulative VP held by all players in a tribe. Prize
distribution system TBC.

Expected Result: A longer season (6-8 weeks) with more casual, long-term strategic gameplay. Longer timeframe allows a
broader window for game revenue and socials attention. Blitz games can run concurrently, but series games will be held
until seasonal breaks and Eternum will be the focus of promotion.

New Map Layer Permitting Faster Travel

Problem: Map traversal has been an issue in previous seasons. Players struggle to get their armies in range to contest a
hyperstructure before the owner wins the game. Building portals or teleporters isn’t the ideal solution, because it puts
the ability to fast travel in the hands of the few and leads to potential imbalances.

Solution: The proposed system intends to provide a universal alternative layer to the game world, accessible by
interacting with a new static world structure (‘spire’) that teleports the interacting army to a new map layer. These
spires are placed at regular intervals around the world map such that armies from any Realm can get to one within a
couple of real-world days. The spires are reflected in the new layer, but they appear much closer together (i.e. fewer
hexes between them). While movement physics remain the same, the result is that travelling one hex in the new layer is
equivalent to travelling multiple hexes in the world map. Armies can interact with spires again in the new layer to
return to the standard map.

Requirements: Lore, aesthetics and naming New world structure ‘spire’ Consideration of gameplay within the new layer
(spacing, exploration & rewards, etc.) Spacing and Movement Design Distance between Realms in the main map layer is
changed from 10 to 15. Distance between spires in the main layer vs. the new layer is 15:1. Player armies travel to the
new layer by interacting with an adjacent spire. They appear in the new layer in the corresponding tile to the one they
interacted from in the base layer. Every 6th Realm slot is replaced with a spire - 90 tiles from spire-to-spire, 89
between. In the new layer, spires are placed in the same pattern every 6 tiles, 5 between. Once teleported to the new
layer, a player army would only need to travel 4-5 tiles to be able to interact with the next spire. There is only one
biome in the alt layer (‘Ethereal Plains’) which confers no combat or stamina bonuses/penalties to any troop type. All
exploration requires 30 stamina, and all travel costs 20 stamina. There is only one discoverable world structure to be
found when exploring this layer - Bitcoin Mines. There are no exploration rewards in the alt-layer. System needed to
prevent armies from blocking tiles, suggest combat across layers: Army X on the world map interacts with a spire, but
the corresponding tile in the new layer has Army Y on it. The player of Army X gets a combat menu and combat can be
initiated with Army Y in the same way as adjacent armies on the world map. The same would occur in the reverse situation
where Army Y tries to come through to the world map.

Example Flow of Movement Design: On the main map, orange and purple hexes represent Realm and spire placement
respectively. An army on the red hex interacts with a spire.

The army is moved to the corresponding tile on the new layer (yellow), it then travels 5 tiles to arrive adjacent to the
next spire (which is only 6 tiles away in the new layer).

The army interacts with the next spire and is moved back to the corresponding tile on the main layer.

The result is that the army has travelled 89 tiles, but only moved 5 tiles (in the alt layer). We assume that
interacting with a spire and teleporting to the alt layer is a normal travel movement and costs 20 stamina.

Expected Result: Literally making the game 3D chess. Players will fight for control of both layers, and due to the
close-quarters nature of the new layer it will be a hotspot for tribal drama and combat. It will also encourage
competition in the late game, avoiding situations where an attempt to attack a game winning hyperstructure is futile due
to distance.

Wonders v.2

Problem: The few Wonder Realms in existence don’t feel special. S0 gave wonders a donkey production bonus that broke the
gas market of the game, while S1 simply boosted production a little.

Solution: Allow wonder owners to compete within their own religion-themed metagame, incentivised by a portion of the
overall season prize pool. Wonders become their own religious states with the goal of accumulating ‘faith’ points from
‘faithful’ followers. These points are separate to VP, just as wonder followings are separate to tribal alliances. Each
owner of a Realm or Village can become faithful to a wonder voluntarily. This system can provide an avenue to reward
influencers within a game of Eternum, while providing a religion-style gameplay system.

Requirements: Add a separate ‘faith’ leaderboard and create an end-of-season prize pool for wonder players and their
followers. Distribute a share of the prize between the wonder owner and their followers proportional to their
accumulated faith points (FP). Allow players to devote their Realms and/or Villages to a selected wonder by becoming
‘faithful’. Holy Sites are a new world structure discoverable when exploring the world map. Once a Holy Site is
discovered and captured by a player, it can become faithful to a wonder. The FP emitted by a wonder is dependent on the
number of Realms and Villages that are faithful to it. All emitted FP are totalised and determine placement on the faith
leaderboard. The wonder Realm itself produces a baseline 50 FP per second. Faithful structures increase this as follows:
50 FP/sec for a faithful Holy Site 10 FP/sec for a faithful Realm 1 FP/sec for a faithful Village Wonder Realms can be
faithful to other wonders, increasing the FP of the followed wonder by 50 per second. This decision causes the
submitting wonder to forfeit all their followers and relinquish their 50 baseline FP/sec to the wonder they are faithful
to. Subservient wonders can no longer attract the faith of other Realms and Villages and they no longer accumulate FP to
themselves on the faith leaderboard. The wonder owner and owners of all faithful Holy Sites/Realms/Villages gain a share
of the emitted FP, these are accumulated in a player’s wallet, similarly to VP. The owner receives 30%, while followers
receive the other 70% split between all faithful based on the FP that they contribute to the cause. For example, a
wonder (50 FP) has another wonder (50 FP), two Holy Sites (100 FP), 3 Realms (30 FP), and 10 Villages (10 FP) faithful
to it. 240 FP are accumulated by the wonder on the faith leaderboard per second. The wallet of the wonder owner receives
30% (72 FP), the remaining 168 FP is divided between contributors according to their weighting: Owner - (50/240)*168 =
35 (on top of their 30%) Subservient wonder owner - (50/240)*168 = 35 Each Holy Site owner - (50/240)*168 = 35 Each
Realm owner - (10/240)*168 = 7 Each Village owner - (1/240)\*168 = 0.7 Faith points are unique to each wonder, players
can accumulate faith points for multiple wonders by having different Realms or Villages faithful to different wonders.
Switching religions is discouraged as faith point accumulation takes time (late converters jumping on the winning wonder
will have low accumulated FP compared to long-term followers). Wonder Realms can be captured, the new owner claims all
the potential benefits that the wonder provides, including all faithful Realms & Villages (their faith isn’t reset on
wonder capture, but owners can change faith if they don’t wish to support the new owner). The original owner still
maintains their faith points accumulated so far. At the end of the game, the $LORDS prize pool for the faith leaderboard
is distributed amongst the top ranking wonders. The wonder owner receives 30% of the allocation, and the remaining 70%
is distributed to all wallets that hold FP in that wonder based on the weighting of their FP.

Expected Result: Wonders become the game pieces of influencers and cult leaders, tapping into the social dynamics of the
crypto space. Social leaders are incentivised to participate both by the prize pool allocation and the potential to grow
their followings across seasons of Eternum. Faith points act as a measure of loyalty and status within a wonder
following, which owners may use to further reward and incentivise their followings outside of the game.

Additional Features (pushed to S3): Tithes, wonder treasuries, temples (and associated upgrades) in Realms, and
blessings.

Improving Villages and Army Deployment

Problems: The first iteration of Villages in Eternum S1 was largely successful as a means of player onboarding, however
some of the mechanics were abused by more hardcore players and tribes: ‘Village bombing’ allowed players to buy Villages
next to their opponents and immediately deploy their starting army to attack an exposed Realm. Preventing villages from
attacking their parent Realm is not a solution because it leads to many other balance and mechanic issues. Players also
deployed large armies from Villages placed on captured frontline Realms, allowing risk-free deployment as Villages can’t
be captured. Allowing Villages to be captured or otherwise subjugated reduces their appeal as an effective, casual
onboarding tool. Villages could be made unplayable by continuous raids draining resources and troops. This wasn’t
commonly reported and Realms should be able to defend themselves from hostile Villages through frequent raids to limit
their military capacity, however complete nullification could turn people off the game.

Solutions: Lock starting troops for Villages behind the end of a timer. Limit army strength deployable based on the
level of a Village/Realm. This will require a new army ‘strength’ system. Give Villages partial immunity from subsequent
raids after a successful raid. Restrict raiding to only target troops while this immunity is active.

Requirements: Give Villages a 48-hour raid immunity timer from the moment they are settled. Villages don’t have
immediate access to starting troops, they can instead claim starting troops 24 hours after settling by pressing a button
to ‘call militia’. Disable the ability to claim deposits until after the immunity timer ends. New system - Army
Strength. This is a simple calculation that can be used in future mechanics while also giving players a simple visual
indication of the strength of an army at a glance. Army*Strength = Troop_Amount * Tier*Strength Tier_Strength: T1 = 1,
T2 = 3, T3 = 9. Limit the strength of deployable armies (including guard armies) based on Realm/Village level, each of
which has a deployment cap: Settlement - 6k, City - 30k, Kingdom - 90k, Empire - 180k. The maximum size of an army is
dependent on the deployment cap and the tier strength of the troops being deployed, such that: Max_Army_Size =
(Deployment_Cap / Tier_Strength) * Tier_Modifier Tier_Modifier: T1 = 0.5, T2 = 1.0, T3 = 1.5 Remove the previous 100k
max army size cap in favour of the new strength caps. Remove the +1 additional army slot per military building and
instead increase the number of army slots based on Realm/Village level: Settlement - 1 field, 1 guard City - 3 field, 2
guard Kingdom - 5 field, 3 guard Empire - 8 field, 4 guard Add ‘buildings constructed’ requirement to Realm/Village
upgrades. Players must build up their Realms to support large armies. Give Villages immunity from being raided for
resources for 24h after a successful raid, troop stocks can be pillaged without restriction. Maintain and solidify
mechanics that prevent the transfer of troops to Villages from outside sources.

Expected Results: The introduction of the immunity and troop-claim timers prevents instant ‘Village bombing’ tactics, as
any attempts will be highly telegraphed. Army strength provides a simple system to display and compare the relative
strengths of armies. This also feeds into a system that limits the number of armies and their size to the level of the
Realm or Village. Each upgrade level has a deployment cap that restricts the number (and therefore strength) of troops
per army. Example 1: A Kingdom (90k cap) wishes to deploy a T2 army. (90k / 3) _ 1 = 30k is the maximum number of T2
troops in an army from that Realm. Example 2: A City (30k cap) wants to deploy a T3 army. (30k / 9) _ 1.5 = 5k T3 troops
per army. This system has a few benefits: Encourage investment in Realm/Village upgrades. The strongest armies can only
be deployed from Empires. Prevent the use of villages as risk-free forward operating bases. Army sizes are limited as
Villages can only be upgraded once, so this tactic becomes less effective. The tier modifier encourages investment in
higher tier troops, as more army strength can be deployed at higher tiers. This simulates improved command efficiency
with a smaller force. Additional notes: Removal of the +1 additional army per military building, combined with buffing
the army allowance per Realm/Village level, prevents Village owners from spamming military buildings for extra armies
while encouraging actual resource investment. Since Villages can’t be captured, Realm owners need to be able to nullify
them as a military threat through ongoing raids. However, we don’t want Realm owners to lock Village players out of the
game completely through relentless raiding. Making resources, labor and donkeys immune for 24h after a successful raid,
while leaving troops open to continuous pillaging, allows Realm owners to nullify the military threat while still
allowing the Village player to participate in the game economy.

Agents Evolved

Problems: With only one agent system in S1, higher-tier agent armies were very strong and potentially menacing when
influenced by players through instant global chat systems. Agents were also unable to explore and had limited incentives
to interact with the world beyond fabricated desires in their prompts. Agents also had a single reward scale which meant
that hunting high-tier agents wasn’t worth the effort.

Solutions: Separate agents into two or more types with different gameplay systems, characteristics, and stakes. Solve
global communication through the use of transactional requirements for sending messages to agents.

Requirements: Basic agents: Weak T1 armies with 200 - 1,000 troops Spawn in the same manner as Loot Crates currently do
in Blitz - provided the game contract has capacity for new basic agents, exploring a tile has a chance to spawn a basic
agent on a random, nearby explored tile. Generally hostile. They can communicate with players but don’t really have any
directives beyond survival, accumulating resources, essence and $LORDS. Due to their low army strength, they won’t be
much of a threat to active Realms players, but will be relevant for casuals and Village players. May attack players
and/or other agents. Primarily designed to make the world feel populated and will create a minigame for players to
‘police’ their lands. Can move on explored tiles, attack field armies, and raid world structures. Spawn with a small
amount of $LORDS in their inventory (5-10 tokens) Advanced agents: Are much stronger, T2 & T3 armies with 1,000 - 2,500
troops. Are discovered by exploring the world map, they remain in ‘stasis’ - a bound state - rendering them unable to
move, attack, or communicate until attacked or ‘awakened’ by an adjacent army. Have deeper personalities and more
complex directives, they may be friendly, hostile, negotiable and/or deceitful. May attack players and/or other agents.
Designed to add flavour to the world and allow players to converse and interact with them as NPCs. Can move on explored
tiles, explore new tiles, attack, and raid world structures. Spawn with a moderate amount of $LORDS in their inventory
(15-30 tokens) Ethereal agents: Are scarily strong, T3 armies with 2,500 - 5,000 troops. Unique ‘Ethereal’ skin. Are
discovered by exploring the Ethereal layer and are free to move and attack from the moment of discovery. Always hostile,
but will prioritise survival - attacking weaker forces and under-defended Bitcoin Mines. Cannot negotiate or be reasoned
with, their lore is that they wish to drive ‘the impure’ from their dimension. They’ll accumulate materials (including
Bitcoin) from anyone they defeat and won’t attack other Ethereal agents. Intended to restrict access to the Ethereal
layer (and the Bitcoin within), ensuring it is reserved for high–tier players and organised groups. Can move on explored
Ethereal layer tiles, attack, and raid Bitcoin Mines. Spawn with the most amount of $LORDS in their inventory (40-100
tokens). Restrict all agents from using spires, they cannot travel between layers like players can. Communications
system and ruleset to ensure that agents can only receive communications from entities in their local area. Messages to
agents are now transactions which must be sent from a field army. Each message requires an amount of essence to send.
The greater the distance between the army and the agent, the more essence is required. Essence Cost =round max 0, 5000\*

Expected Results: The separation of agent armies into multiple ‘tiers’ will allow for greater variety and design
flexibility when it comes to populating the world with NPC forces and distributing rewards amongst them. Casual and
small scale players will have a rewarding experience with basic agents, while advanced agents will be meaningful playing
pieces on the game map. The revised spawn system for basic agents will ensure a continuous ‘pop-up’ presence of minor
agents, so areas will never really be fully cleared. Since advanced agents are found when revealing unexplored tiles and
must first be activated by an adjacent army, they don’t pose an instant threat and can be nullified if a player doesn’t
wish to engage with them. However, once activated their ability to explore the map will potentially make them more
influential and relevant in local power struggles. The changes to communications rules will generally protect players
against global comms spam. Where players could previously influence agents from an unlimited distance, transactional
messaging will place a real cost on attempts to influence agents from a distance.

Optional Explore Rewards

Problem: The current version of the game allows troops to collect and carry exploration rewards automatically, however
once the army is at their weight capacity, they can no longer explore and need to return to a Realm to transfer their
inventory before exploring again. This disincentivises exploration and creates logistical issues when travelling long
distances.

Solution: Allow armies to bypass the requirement to collect exploration rewards, instead allowing the rewards to be left
on the tile and collected by another army with inventory space.

Requirements: Develop contracts and client to handle all hexes having a check to determine if exploration rewards have
been collected by an army or not. Make adjustments to army exploration and inventory mechanics, ensuring that armies can
still explore when at full carry capacity. An army requires a minimum of 50 troops to explore a tile. Develop a UI
indicator for hexes with materials remaining in storage, this should be very subtle.

Expected Results: The changes described should remove some barriers to ongoing exploration and long-distance travel.

Other Features

Day/Night Cycles Already implemented. Visual feature only.

New Building: Artificer’s Workshop A new building that facilitates the production of relics. Players can pay a
combination of food, essence and $LORDS to produce a weightless, non-tradable resource called ‘research’. Production
cost of research is detailed in the Eternum Production Balance sheet. 50,000 research can be burned in exchange for a
randomised relic, the weighting of relic draw chances is TBC.

New World Structure: Camp Implemented similarly to Blitz. Acts as a playable structure but: Can’t be upgraded beyond
settlement level, Has access to a single resource, randomly drawn according to rarity rating, Produces at the same rate
as Villages (i.e. 50% the rate of Realms). Discovery chance when exploring is 1/250. Subject to the same army deployment
rules for settlement level structures as defined in the Improving Villages and Army Deployment section.

New World Structure: Essence Rift Implemented similarly to Blitz. Is a non-playable structure that produces 1
essence/sec indefinitely. Discovery chance when exploring is 1/125.

New World Structure: Holy Site As described in the Wonders v.2 section. Is a non-playable structure that can be devoted
to a wonder, producing 50 FP/sec indefinitely. Discovery chance when exploring is 1/1000.

New World Structure: Bitcoin Mine A new world structure which allows players to exhibit ‘proof of work’ to extract
sponsored wBTC from the game. Bitcoin mines are only discoverable in the Ethereal layer, with a 1/50 chance of being
discovered when exploring a tile. Players must first capture the Bitcoin Mine from bandits and claim the structure. The
defenders are T3 (in line with Ethereal Agents). Donkeys can’t travel to the Ethereal layer, so all materials
transported in and out must be carried by armies. Labor can be transported to the mine and used to produce ‘work’ - a
weightless and non-transferrable material. There are five levels of production and each produces a different amount of
‘work’. Very Low = 1 labor/second = 1 work/second Low = 2 labor/second = 2 work/second Medium = 3 labor/second = 3
work/second High = 4 labor/second = 4 work/second Very High = 5 labor/second = 5 work/second At the end of each phase
(every 10 minutes), the total amount of work in that phase is calculated and each mine is attributed a contribution
percentage. A roll is performed and the Bitcoin reward in the form of yet another new material - Satoshis - for that
phase is sent to the mine that wins the roll. For example, there are 3 mines and they produce 50%, 30% and 20% of the
total work, ordered from highest to lowest. A roll between 0 and 100 is performed: 42 is rolled. Mine 1 wins as the roll
was between 0-50. If instead 69 is rolled. Mine 2 wins as the roll was between 50-80. The same number of Satoshis are
emitted every phase throughout the game as long as there is work being produced, the amount is TBD based on the amount
of sponsored BTC and the expected length of the season. Weight of Satoshis is also TBD (dependent on the number of
Satoshis per phase). Aesthetically, Satoshis appear as golden, pixelated metal coins that radiate fading pixels - it’s
otherworldly and foreign to the general Realms art style. As such, donkeys want nothing to do with it. They can’t
transport Satoshis like they can any other material in the game, even when it has been returned to the main layer.
Instead, it must be transferred to an army and carried across the map to a Realm owned by a player, from which it can be
bridged out of the game. This system is designed to replicate the POW concepts of Bitcoin as closely as possible in a
gamified way. It produces an additional sink for labor (and therefore resources), as well as a new gameplay element with
stakes.
